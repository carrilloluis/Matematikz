Sea : \qquad\qquad$f(x) = \dfrac{(x + 4)^2}{x + 3}$\qquad Hallar : \qquad$f'(x)$ \\ \\

\noindent{\scriptsize Solución} \\
Considerando que : $f(x) = \dfrac{u}{v} \implies f'(x) = \dfrac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}$ \\ \\

Por tanto en : \qquad$f(x) = \dfrac{(x + 4)^2}{x + 3}$
\begin{eqnarray*}
u & = & (x + 4)^2 \\
u' & = & 2 (x + 4)(1) \\
v & = & x + 3 \\
v' & = & 1
\end{eqnarray*}

\begin{eqnarray*}
f'(x) &=& \dfrac{2(x + 4)(x + 3) - (x+4)^2 (1)}{(x+3)^2} \\
f'(x) &=& \dfrac{(x + 4)[2(x + 3) - (x + 4)]}{(x + 3)^2} \\
f'(x) &=& \dfrac{(x + 4)(2x + 6 - x - 4)}{(x + 3)^2} \\
f'(x) &=& \dfrac{(x + 4)(x + 2)}{(x +3)^2} = \dfrac{x^{2} + 6x + 8}{x^{2} + 6x + 9}
\end{eqnarray*}

{\scriptsize Otra posible solución}
\begin{eqnarray*}
f'(x) &=& \dfrac{2(x + 4)(x + 3) - (x+4)^2 (1)}{(x+3)^{2}} \\
f'(x) &=& \dfrac{2(x + 4)(x + 3)}{(x+3)^{2}} - \dfrac{(x+4)^{2}}{(x+3)^{2}} \\
f'(x) &=& \dfrac{2x + 8}{(x+3)} - \dfrac{(x+4)^{2}}{(x+3)^{2}} \\
f'(x) &=& \dfrac{2x + 8}{(x+3)} - \Bigg[\dfrac{(x+4)}{(x+3)}\Bigg]^{2} 
\end{eqnarray*}
